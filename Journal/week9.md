During Week 9, our team moved from the design phase to the practical implementation phase of the hospital network project. In Week 8, we successfully completed the full hospital network design in Cisco Packet Tracer, including the network topology, VLANs, IP addressing, and device configurations. After demonstrating the simulation to our mentor, we received approval to proceed with building the prototype using physical networking devices.

At the beginning of Week 9, our mentor granted us access to the networking laboratory and provided all the required equipment, including routers, switches, wireless access points, Ethernet cables, and other supporting devices. Before starting the setup, our team discussed how we would replicate the Packet Tracer design in the lab and planned the placement and connections of each device.

We then started assembling the prototype in the computer lab. During this session, we successfully set up one section of the hospital network and established basic connectivity between the devices. This was an important step because it confirmed that our design could be implemented in a real hardware environment.

While working with the physical devices, we faced several challenges. Some connections did not work properly at first, and we experienced issues with cable placement, port assignments, and initial device configurations. We had to troubleshoot these problems by checking connections and adjusting settings until the devices communicated correctly.

Due to the limited lab time, we were not able to complete all remaining configurations and tests. However, Week 9 was a significant milestone, as we began the practical implementation of the project and successfully created the first working prototype of the hospital network. This progress provides a strong foundation for completing the full setup in the upcoming weeks.
