Overview

The first three weeks of the project were mainly focused on understanding what we were expected to do and trying to decide on a suitable topic. In the beginning, our team was not very clear about the direction of the project, which made it difficult to move forward. We spent most of this time discussing ideas and trying to figure out what would be best for us.

Challenges

During the first two weeks, we faced several difficulties that slowed down our progress. One of the main issues was selecting a project topic, as many of the ideas we came up with were already chosen by other groups. This made us repeatedly go back and rethink our choices. Along with that, it was also hard to manage everyone’s time since all team members had different schedules, and we struggled to find a common time to meet and discuss properly. Because of these reasons, we were not able to make much progress in the initial weeks.

Progress

By the third week, we made a stronger effort to organise ourselves and move forward. The team came together, discussed seriously, and finally agreed on a project topic. Once the topic was finalised, we started working more efficiently. Each team member selected their role, and we had a meeting with our mentor to get feedback and guidance. Even though we were slightly behind schedule, we managed to complete the proposal based on our understanding and submitted it by the end of Week 3.

Reflection

Looking back at the first three weeks, we realised that communication and time management are very important in a team project. The initial confusion and delays taught us how important it is to make decisions early and stay organised. Even though we faced challenges in the beginning, we were able to work together, overcome the issues, and successfully complete our proposal.
