# Overview

In the first three weeks of the project, we mostly focused on understanding what we needed to do and deciding on a good topic. At the start, our team wasn’t really sure about the project direction, so it was hard to make progress. We spent a lot of time just talking about different ideas and trying to figure out what would work best for us.

# Challenges

During the first two weeks, we had some problems that slowed us down. Picking a topic was tough because many ideas we thought of were already taken by other groups. This meant we had to keep going back and rethinking our options. Also, it was hard to find a time that worked for everyone since we all had different schedules. Because of this, we didn’t get much done in those weeks.

# Progress

By the third week, we tried harder to get organised and move ahead. The team had serious discussions and finally agreed on a project topic. After that, we started working better. Everyone chose their roles, and we met with our mentor to get advice and feedback. Even though we were a bit behind schedule, we finished the proposal based on what we understood and submitted it by the end of Week 3.

# Reflection

Looking back, we learned that good communication and managing time well are really important for group projects. The confusion and delays in the beginning showed us why it’s important to make decisions early and stay organised. Even though we had some struggles at first, we worked together and managed to finish our proposal successfully.
